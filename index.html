--- 2015, 2017 LaPrimaire.org, co-Initiator | The Open Primary Election LaPrimaire.org is a free, online, primary election designed to crowdsource the best candidate to run for the 2017 french Presidential Election by way of a 100% democratic and transparent process. Started with David Guez in 2015, we organized LaPrimaire.org in France in 2016 with 215 candidates, 110,000 participating citizens and $135.450 raised from 3,518 donors.
A candidate was elected and gathered 135 pledges from town mayors in only 45 days, a record time but not fast enough to be on the ballot.

For LaPrimaire.org, I developed one of the 1st complex chat bot based on Telegram bot API and created the Giskard framework out of it. I co-authored a research paper (not published) explaining the science behind the random election process we invented. I also help design and implement cocorico, a voting module on Ethereum that was developed for LaPrimaire.org. Finally, another non-profit solely dedicated to promoting the Majority Judgement voting mechanism was spun-off from this experience. We also invented a new drug to fix democracy: Democratol :)
